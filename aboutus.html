About Us

Hear Her Out is a social organization dedicated to give females a voice and an environment to talk about the abuse and violence they have been facing. The organization strives to raise awareness of issues such as sexual assault, harassment, rape etc., that have been happening within our circles and neighbourhood. It is for survivors of violence who are in need of a voice. A voice that needs to be heard in order to raise awareness and strengthen security to lessen sexual assault and harassment that has been happening within our circles. We want to give females, especially our age, a space to talk about the issues they are facing. As it is a very critical and sensitive topic, they can do it anonymously through our google form that has been marketed on our social media and around the university campus. We are currently focused on expanding in Leeds and Delhi, However we will be working on expanding to other universities as well.

The key idea behind story sharing is that it can be healing, calming, and therapeutic. It brings awareness to others about sexual violence that happens so often and is often overlooked. Lastly, it strengthens the security in society. This organization currently also provides knowledge about consent, sexual assault vs sexual harassment, domestic abuse etc. to educate about the offenses.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               

Our Goals for the Future

For the future, we are planning to being potential keynote speakers, that have already been lined up for webinars, from UCLA, Stanford, and Delhi University to talk about violence against women and girls. In addition to that we are in the process of collaborating with other foundations and NGOS.

Lastly, in the near future and our main goal for this organisation is to provide free therapy sessions for the survivors by partnering online with licensed therapy organisations. There are many girls of our age who are ashamed to talk about the traumatizing experience they faced with their parents and they are looked down upon for starting therapy, they feel unheard and thus, we want to bring therapy sessions for them to be able to feel like their voice is heard.

The next steps for this organisation would be to market the "Story form" to as many people as possible so they can have access to voice their experiences anonymously. Along with that, we plan on gathering volunteers to outreach and reach out to guest speakers for interactive and influential interviews. Lastly, we want to research and plan fundraising and establish partnerships with therapy organizations.
